Major Compound Words Databases
LADEC (Large Database of English Compounds)
The largest academic database specifically for compound words contains over 8,000 English compound words that can be parsed into two constituents. The database includes:

8,376 unique compounds with additional parsing variants

Available in both CSV and Stata formats

Hand-coded compounds with linguistic and psycholinguistic variables

Originally available through the University of Alberta's repository, though the specific download link has been migrated to https://ualberta.scholaris.ca

Comprehensive Word Lists for General Use
While not exclusively compound words, the dwyl/english-words GitHub repository provides extensive word lists:

479,000+ English words in multiple formats

Available files include:

words.txt - contains all words

words_alpha.txt - alphabetic characters only

words_dictionary.json - JSON format for programming use

uk-us-dict.txt - UK/US spelling differences

Key Points
None of the major compound word datasets (including LADEC and dwyl/english-words) provide compound words already divided into syllables.

LADEC is the most comprehensive, research-grade resource for English compound words, but it only provides compounds split into their constituent words, not into syllables.

Most educational lists and open-source repositories focus on listing compound words or their constituent parts, not their syllabic structure.

Syllable Division Guidance
Standard practice for syllable division in compound words is to:

Split the compound into its constituent words (e.g., "butterfly" → "butter" + "fly").

Divide each constituent word into syllables using general English syllabication rules (e.g., "butter" → "but-ter", "fly" → "fly"; so "butterfly" → "but-ter-fly").

There are several well-established rules and online tools for syllable division, but these are typically applied programmatically or with the help of syllabification libraries, not via pre-annotated datasets.

Recommendations for Your App
Best starting point: Use the LADEC database to reliably identify and split compound words into their constituent parts.

For syllable division: Integrate an English syllabification library or API (such as those used by sites like HowManySyllables.com) to break each constituent word into syllables.

No ready-made dataset currently provides both compound word lists and their syllable breakdowns. Combining LADEC with a syllabification tool will yield the best results for your app.
