Moby Hyphenator 

Moby Hyphenator Overview 

The Moby Hyphenator is part of the Moby Project, a collection of public-domain lexical resources created by Grady Ward. It provides hyphenation data for over 185,000 English words, making it one of the largest freely available resources for syllable division in plain text. 

Key Features 

Format: The data consists of English words with syllable breaks indicated by a special character (typically a bullet or hyphen). For example, "at- mos- phere" or "in-ten-tion-al." 

Coverage: Contains hyphenation for 187,175 words and phrases, including many common and uncommon English words. 

Encoding: The original files use MacRoman encoding, and syllable breaks are marked with a bullet character (decimal 165, hexadecimal A5), though some entries may use hyphens as well. 

No Diacritics: The dataset does not use phonetic symbols, schwas, or diacritics—syllables are divided using regular letters, making it suitable for educational and accessibility applications. 

Public Domain: The entire dataset is in the public domain, free for any use, including commercial and educational projects.
