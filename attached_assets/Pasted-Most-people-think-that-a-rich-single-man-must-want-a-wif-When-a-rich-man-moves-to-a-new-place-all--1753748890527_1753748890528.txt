Most people think that a rich single man must want a wif. When a rich man moves to a new place, all the families think he should marry one of their daughter. Mrs. Bennet came to her husband one da. She had big new. "My dear M. Bennet," she said, "have you heard? Someone rented Netherfield Park at last!" Mr. Bennet said he had not heard this new. "But it is true!" she sai. "Mrs. Long just came her. She told me all about i." Mr. Bennet did not say anythin. "Do you not want to know who rented it?" his wife aske. She was getting upse. "You want to tell me," he sai. "I do not mind hearing i." This was all she needed to hea. "My dear, you must know what Mr. Long told m. A young man with lots of money rented Netherfiel. He is from the north of Englan. He came on Monday in a fancy carriage to see the plac. He liked it so much that he agreed to rent it right awa. He will move in before fal. Some of his workers will be in the house by next wee." "What is his name?" Mr. Bennet aske. "Bingley." "Is he married or single?" "Oh! Single, my dear! A single man with lots of mone. He has four or five thousand a yea. What a good thing for our girls!" "How so? How can this help them?" "My dear M. Bennet," his wife said, "how can you be so difficult! You must know that I am thinking of him marrying one of the." "Is that why he is moving here?" "Moving here to marry? That is silly! But he may fall in love with one of the. So you must visit him as soon as he come." "I do not need to do tha. You and the girls may g. Or you may send them alon. That might be bette. You are as pretty as any of the. Mr. Bingley may like you the bes." "My dear, you are too kin. I was pretty onc. But I do not think I am special no. When a woman has five grown daughters, she should stop thinking about her own beaut." "In cases like that, a woman does not have much beauty left to think abou." "But, my dear, you must go and see M. Bingley when he come." "I cannot promise tha." "But think about your daughter. Think what a good marriage it would be for one of the. Sir William and Lady Lucas will go visit hi. They want their daughters to meet him to. They do not usually visit new peopl. You must g. We cannot visit him if you do not go firs." "You worry too muc. I am sure M. Bingley will be happy to see yo. I will send a note with yo. I will tell him he can marry any of the girls he want. But I will put in a good word for my little Lizz." "Do not do such a thing! Lizzy is not better than the other. She is not as pretty as Jan. She is not as fun as Lydi. But you always like her bes." "None of them have much to offer," he sai. "They are all silly like other girl. But Lizzy is a bit smarter than her sister." "Mr. Bennet, how can you talk about your own children this way? You like to upset m. You do not care about my poor nerve." "You are wrong, my dea. I respect your nerves very muc. They are old friends of min. You have talked about them for twenty year."
