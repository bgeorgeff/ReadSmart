Everyone knows that a rich single man must want a wif. When a man like this moves to a new place, people think he will marry one of the local girl. Mrs. Bennet said to her husband, "My dear M. Bennet, have you heard? Someone has rented Netherfield Park at last!" Mr. Bennet said he had not hear. "But it is true," she sai. "Mrs. Long just told me all about i." "Do you not want to know who rented it?" she aske. "You want to tell m. I will listen," he sai. Mrs. Bennet was excite. She said, "Mrs. Long says a young man with lots of money rented i. He came from the north of Englan. He came last Monda. He liked the house so much that he made a deal with M. Morris right awa. His servants will come next wee." "What is his name?" Mr. Bennet aske. "Bingley." "Is he married or single?" "Oh! Single, my dear! A single man with lots of mone. What good news for our girls!" "How does this help them?" he aske. "My dear M. Bennet," she said, "I am thinking he might marry one of the." "Is that why he moved here?" "Of course not! But he might fall in love with one of the. So you must visit him when he gets her." "I do not need to do tha. You and the girls can g. Or just send the girl. Maybe M. Bingley will like you best of al." "My dear, you are too kin. I was pretty onc. But I am not special no. When a woman has five grown daughters, she should stop thinking about her own look." "But you must go see M. Bingley when he comes," she sai. "I do not promise to do that," he sai. "But think of your daughters! Think what a good marriage one of them could make! Sir William and Lady Lucas will go just for that reaso. You must go to. We cannot visit him if you do not go firs." "You worry too muc. I am sure M. Bingley will be happy to see yo. I will write him a not. I will tell him he can marry any of our girls he want. But I will put in a good word for my little Lizz." "Do not do that! Lizzy is not better than the other. She is not as pretty as Jan. She is not as fun as Lydi. But you always like her bes." "None of them are very special," he sai. "They are all silly like other girl. But Lizzy is a bit smarter than her sister." "Mr. Bennet, how can you talk about your own children that way? You like to upset m. You do not care about my poor nerve." "You are wrong, my dea. I care very much about your nerve. They are old friends of min. You have talked about them for twenty year."
