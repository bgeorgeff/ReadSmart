veryone knows that a rich single man must want a wif. All the families around think he should marry one of their daughter. "My dear M. Bennet," said his wife one da. "Did you hear that Netherfield Park has a new renter?" Mr. Bennet said he had not heard this new. "But it is true," she sai. "Mrs. Long came here and told me all about i." "Do you want to know who rented it?" his wife aske. "You want to tell m. I will listen," he sai. Mrs. Bennet told him, "A young rich man from the north rented Netherfiel. He came on Monda. He loved the hous. He made a deal with M. Morris right awa. He will move in before Michaelma. His servants will be there next wee." "What is his name?" "Bingley." "Is he married or single?" "Oh! Single, my dear! A single man with lots of mone. He has four or five thousand a yea. What good news for our girls!" "How so? How does this help them?" "How can you be so difficult! You know I want him to marry one of the." "Is that why he moved here?" "Of course no. But he might fall in love with one of the. So you must visit him when he come." "I do not need to do tha. You and the girls can g. Or send them alon. That might be bette. You are as pretty as any of the. Mr. Bingley might like you bes." "My dear, you are too kin. I was pretty onc. But I am not special no. When a woman has five grown daughters, she should stop thinking about her own look." "In such cases, a woman does not have much beauty left to think abou." "But my dear, you must go see M. Bingley when he comes to the are." "I cannot promise tha." "Think about your daughter. What a good match it would be for one of the. Sir William and Lady Lucas will go visit hi. They usually do not visit new peopl. You must go to. We cannot visit him if you do not go firs." "You worry too muc. Mr. Bingley will be happy to see yo. I will send a note with yo. I will tell him he can marry any of our girls he want. But I will put in a good word for my little Lizz." "Do not do that! Lizzy is not better than the other. She is not as pretty as Jan. She is not as fun as Lydi. But you always like her bes." "None of them are very special," he sai. "They are all silly like other girl. But Lizzy is a bit smarter than her sister." "Mr. Bennet, how can you say such mean things about your own children? You like to upset m. You do not care about my poor nerve." "You are wrong, my dea. I respect your nerves very muc. They are old friends of min. You have talked about them for twenty year."
