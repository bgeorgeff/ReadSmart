Everyone knows that a rich single man must want a wif. When he moves to a new place, all the families think he might marry one of their daughter. Mrs. Bennet said to her husband one day, "My dear M. Bennet, did you hear that someone rented Netherfield Park?" Mr. Bennet said he had not heard this new. "But it is true," she sai. "Mrs. Long told me all about i." "Do you want to know who rented it?" she aske. "You want to tell me, and I will listen," he sai. "Mrs. Long says a young man with lots of money rented i. He comes from the north of Englan. He came on Monday to see the hous. He liked it so much that he said yes right awa. He will move in before fal. Some of his workers will be there next wee." "What is his name?" Mr. Bennet aske. "Bingley." "Is he married or single?" "Oh! Single, my dear! A single man with lots of mone. He has four or five thousand a yea. What good news for our girls!" "How? How does this help them?" "How can you be so silly! You know I am thinking he might marry one of the." "Is that why he is moving here?" "Of course not! But he might fall in love with one of the. So you must visit him when he come." "I do not need to do tha. You and the girls can g. You are as pretty as any of the. Maybe M. Bingley will like you bes." "You are too kin. I was pretty onc. But I do not think I am special no. When a woman has five grown daughters, she should stop thinking about her own look." "In cases like that, a woman does not have much beauty left to think abou." "But my dear, you must go see M. Bingley when he moves her." "I cannot promise tha." "Think about your daughter. Think what a good match this would be for one of the. Sir William and Lady Lucas plan to visit hi. They want their daughters to meet him to. You must g. We cannot visit him if you do not go firs." "I think you worry too muc. Mr. Bingley will be happy to see yo. I will send a note with yo. I will tell him he can marry any of our girls he want. But I will put in a good word for my little Lizz." "Do not do that! Lizzy is not better than the other. She is not as pretty as Jan. She is not as fun as Lydi. But you always like her bes." "None of them are very special," he sai. "They are all silly like other girl. But Lizzy is a bit smarter than her sister." "Mr. Bennet, how can you say such mean things about your own children? You like to upset m. You do not care about my poor nerve." "You are wrong, my dea. I care a lot about your nerve. They are old friends of min. You have talked about them for twenty year."
