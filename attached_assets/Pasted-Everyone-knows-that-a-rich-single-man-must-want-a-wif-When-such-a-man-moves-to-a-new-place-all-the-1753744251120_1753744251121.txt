Everyone knows that a rich single man must want a wif. When such a man moves to a new place, all the families think he might marry one of their daughter. "My dear M. Bennet," Mrs. Bennet said one day, "did you hear that Netherfield Park has a new tenant?" Mr. Bennet said he had not hear. "But it is true," she sai. "Mrs. Long just told me about i." "Do you want to know who took it?" Mrs. Bennet aske. "You want to tell m. I will listen," Mr. Bennet sai. Mrs. Bennet was very excite. She said a rich young man named M. Bingley had rented Netherfiel. He came from the north of Englan. He saw the house on Monday and loved i. He will move in soo. His servants will come next wee. "Is he married or single?" Mr. Bennet aske. "Single! And very rich! What good news for our girls!" Mrs. Bennet sai. "How is that good news?" Mr. Bennet aske. "How can you ask that! I think he might marry one of them," she sai. "Is that why he came here?" he aske. "Of course not! But he might fall in love with one of our girl. You must visit him when he comes," she sai. "I do not need to do tha. You and the girls can g.
